Curriculum

A broad, rigorous curriculum

At Salesian College, academic excellence is at the heart of everything we do. We offer a broad and balanced curriculum across all year groups, taught by expert subject specialists in small classes that allow every pupil to thrive.

In Years 7”“9, pupils follow a wide curriculum covering the core and foundation subjects, designed to build strong foundations and ignite intellectual curiosity. In Years 10 and 11, pupils study for GCSEs across a carefully designed programme that balances breadth with depth.

Our small class sizes — averaging just 10 pupils — mean that teachers know every student, can identify individual strengths and areas for development, and can provide genuinely personalised feedback.

Our approach

How we teach

01

Knowledge-rich teaching

Our curriculum is built on a deep body of subject knowledge. Teachers are subject experts who inspire genuine enthusiasm for their disciplines, going beyond the exam specification to cultivate a love of learning.

02

High expectation for all

We have the same high expectations of every pupil regardless of starting point. Rigorous assessment, detailed feedback, and regular dialogue between teachers and students ensure that no one falls below their potential.

03

Technology-enhanced learning

As an Apple Distinguished School, we integrate technology thoughtfully across the curriculum — using iPads and digital tools to deepen understanding, improve collaboration, and prepare students for a digital world.

Learning & Technology

An Apple Distinguished School

Salesian College has been recognised as an Apple Distinguished School — one of the most prestigious accolades in educational technology — for our innovative and thoughtful use of iPads across the curriculum.

Every pupil uses an iPad as their primary learning device, enabling collaborative projects, digital portfolios, and instant access to a vast range of resources. Teachers use technology to provide timely feedback, track progress, and create engaging, dynamic lessons.
