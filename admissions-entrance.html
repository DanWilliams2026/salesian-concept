Entrance Assessment

About the assessment

What to expect on the day

Salesian College selects pupils on academic ability. Our entrance assessment is designed to identify boys who will thrive in our academic environment and who have the potential to benefit from — and contribute to — the full life of the College.

We want every candidate to feel at ease. The assessment takes place in a calm, well-supervised environment, with staff on hand to welcome families and reassure students on the day.

11+ Assessment (Year 7 entry)

The assessment format

English — 45 minutes

Reading comprehension and creative writing. Tests the ability to understand, analyse, and write with accuracy and imagination.

Mathematics — 45 minutes

Based on the primary curriculum. Tests arithmetic, reasoning, and problem-solving skills appropriate for Year 6.

Verbal Reasoning — 30 minutes

A standardised verbal reasoning paper. Tests the ability to understand and reason using words and language.

SEND support: Candidates who require additional time or other adjustments due to a learning difficulty should inform the admissions office when registering. We are committed to providing appropriate access arrangements.

Assessment day

Registration from 8:30am. Assessment begins at 9:00am and concludes by 12:30pm.

Held at Salesian College, Farnborough. Parents/carers are asked to drop off and collect; there is no waiting area on site.

All materials are provided. Boys should bring a pen, pencil, and rubber. No calculators or dictionaries.

In-year and Sixth Form entry

Students applying to join the school outside of normal Year 7 entry will be assessed individually. This typically involves short tests in English and Mathematics and an interview with the Headmaster or Deputy Head.

Sixth Form applicants are assessed on predicted/achieved GCSE grades plus an interview. Minimum entry requirements apply per subject.
