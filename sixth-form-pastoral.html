Sixth Form Pastoral

Caring for the whole person

More than exam results

The Salesian Sixth Form places the wellbeing of every student at the centre of everything we do. Year 12 and 13 can be among the most demanding and exhilarating years of a young person's life — and we provide the pastoral structures, personal guidance, and genuine community that help students navigate them with confidence.

Every Sixth Former has a personal tutor, access to the school counsellor, and the support of the Head of Sixth Form — who maintains an open-door policy and takes a genuine personal interest in every student's progress and wellbeing.

Personal tutors

Each student is assigned a personal tutor who meets with them regularly to monitor progress, discuss wellbeing, and provide guidance on university applications and future planning.

Wellbeing support

Our school counsellor is available to all students who need confidential support. We take mental health seriously and have a culture where students feel comfortable asking for help.

Community & identity

The Sixth Form common room and regular year group events — from team-building activities to charity fundraisers — foster a genuine sense of community and shared identity.

Study skills

The transition from GCSE to A-level is significant. We offer dedicated study skills sessions and independent learning support to help all students — including external joiners — make that adjustment successfully.

Faith & reflection

The Salesian ethos continues into the Sixth Form. Retreats, reflection days, and opportunities for service ensure that spiritual development and moral growth remain part of the Sixth Form experience.

Celebrating achievement

From UCAS offer holders to sports representation and performing arts — we celebrate every student's achievements through school assemblies, awards ceremonies, and regular recognition events.
