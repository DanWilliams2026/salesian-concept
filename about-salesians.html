The Salesian Order

A mission spanning the globe

The Society of Saint Francis de Sales — the Salesians — is one of the largest religious orders in the Catholic Church, with over 15,000 priests and brothers working in more than 130 countries. Founded by Don Bosco in Turin in 1859, the Order has grown from a handful of workshops and schools in 19th-century Italy into a global network dedicated to the education and pastoral care of young people.

Wherever Salesians go, they bring Don Bosco's Preventive System: an approach to education grounded in reason, religion, and loving kindness. The same values that animate life at Salesian College in Farnborough are lived out in schools, youth centres, and communities across Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Europe.

Global presence

Salesians around the world

Europe

Salesian schools, parishes, and youth centres across Italy, Spain, Portugal, Germany, Poland, and the UK.

Africa

A major presence across sub-Saharan Africa, including schools, vocational training centres, and community development projects.

Latin America

Don Bosco's first missions outside Italy were to Argentina. Latin America remains home to some of the largest Salesian communities in the world.

Asia

Hundreds of schools and youth centres serve millions across India, the Philippines, China, and across South-East Asia.

North America

A strong Salesian presence in the United States and Canada, serving youth communities in some of the most deprived urban areas.

Middle East

Salesian missions serve some of the most vulnerable young people in conflict-affected regions, providing education and hope.

Salesians in Britain

The Salesians in the UK

The Salesians came to England in 1887, establishing schools and parishes across the country. Today, Salesian College Farnborough is part of a broader network of Salesian works in Britain, which includes schools in Battersea and Cheshire as well as parishes and youth ministry programmes.

As part of this wider family, our students benefit from connections with Salesian communities across the country and the world — including the annual Lourdes pilgrimage, which brings together Salesians from across Great Britain.
