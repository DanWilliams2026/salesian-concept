Online safety

e-Safety

Our commitment

Keeping students safe online

The safety and wellbeing of our students — both on and offline — is our highest priority. At Salesian College, e-safety education is woven through the curriculum and pastoral programme from Year 7 upwards, and our managed device environment ensures that students use technology responsibly during the school day.

We work in partnership with parents and carers to promote digital citizenship and safe online behaviour. Our approach is educative rather than simply restrictive — we want students to understand why safe online behaviour matters, not just follow rules.

Managed devices

All student iPads are enrolled in Apple School Manager, with content filtering, app management, and monitoring in place. Inappropriate content is blocked automatically.

Curriculum e-safety

e-Safety is taught in Computing, PSHE, and form time. Topics include cyberbullying, privacy, digital footprint, online relationships, and responsible social media use.

Parent guidance

We provide regular guidance to parents on online safety at home — including recommended settings, conversation starters, and resources from organisations like Childnet and NSPCC.

Report a concern

Something to report?

If you have a concern about a student's online safety — or if a student has experienced cyberbullying, harassment, or has been exposed to harmful content — please contact the school's Designated Safeguarding Lead immediately.
