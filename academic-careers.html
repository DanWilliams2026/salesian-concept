Careers Education

Our approach

Ambitious for every student

At Salesian College, we take careers guidance seriously. Our comprehensive careers education programme — running from Year 7 through to the Sixth Form — is designed to help every student develop self-awareness, explore opportunities, and make confident, informed decisions about their future.

We follow the Gatsby Benchmarks for career guidance and work with a qualified careers adviser, employer partners, and our extensive alumni network to provide students with access to a wide range of experiences and expert guidance.

One-to-one guidance

Every student in Years 10”“13 has access to individual meetings with our qualified careers adviser to explore options and plan their next steps.

Work experience

Year 12 students undertake a structured work experience programme, with placements sourced through our employer partner network and alumni connections.

University preparation

Dedicated UCAS support, personal statement workshops, interview practice, and mock university days prepare Sixth Form students for the application process.

Employer talks

Throughout the year, professionals from medicine, law, engineering, business, media, and the arts visit the school to speak about their careers and sectors.

Enrichment & awards

Students are supported to pursue the Duke of Edinburgh Award, CREST Awards, and other national programmes that develop skills valued by employers and universities.

Alumni mentoring

Our growing alumni network gives students access to mentors working across every major profession — an invaluable resource for informed career exploration.

Gatsby Benchmarks

Salesian College is proud to meet all eight Gatsby Benchmarks for good careers guidance — the nationally recognised framework for high-quality, impartial careers education in schools and colleges.
