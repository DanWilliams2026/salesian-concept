Sixth Form Life

A different kind of sixth form

Independence, ambition, and community

The Salesian Sixth Form is a co-educational community within the school — welcoming both boys from the main school and girls joining in Year 12. Students enjoy greater freedom and responsibility, a dedicated common room and study spaces, and a culture of genuine mutual respect between students and teachers.

Small class sizes, individual attention, and a commitment to each student's future mean that our Sixth Form is not just a place to get exam results — it is a place to become the person you want to be.

A-level subjects

Dedicated spaces

The Sixth Form has its own common room, dedicated study areas, and access to a quiet library space — creating an environment that supports both independent study and social connection.

Co-educational community

The Sixth Form is co-educational, bringing together young men from the main school and young women who join at Year 12. This broadens perspectives and enriches the learning environment for all.

Leadership roles

Sixth Formers take on leadership across school — as Head Students, prefects, mentors, and committee members. These roles develop confidence, communication, and responsibility.

University preparation

From personal statement workshops to Oxford and Cambridge preparation days and mock interviews — our Sixth Form team is wholly committed to helping every student reach their first-choice destination.

Service & enrichment

All Sixth Form students participate in the enrichment programme — volunteering, talks, workshops, and cultural experiences that complement their academic studies and strengthen university applications.

Sport & activities

Sixth Formers continue to participate in sport, performing arts, and clubs — representing the school in fixtures, productions, and competitions alongside younger students.
