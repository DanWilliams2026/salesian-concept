Beyond A-levels

Sixth Form Enrichment

More than three A-levels

Enrichment that sets you apart

The Salesian Sixth Form enrichment programme runs alongside A-level study and is designed to develop the qualities that universities and employers prize most: leadership, service, independent thinking, and resilience. Every Sixth Former participates — because we believe these experiences are not optional extras, but essential parts of a complete education.

Extended Project Qualification (EPQ)

Students research and produce a substantial independent project on a topic of their choice — equivalent to half an A-level and highly regarded by Russell Group universities.

Duke of Edinburgh Gold Award

Year 12 students completing the Gold DofE Award undertake volunteering, skill development, physical challenge, and a residential expedition — one of the UK's most prestigious youth awards.

Work experience

All Year 12 students complete structured work experience, sourced with the support of our employer network and alumni connections across a wide range of industries and professions.

Leadership & service

Head Students, prefects, and student committee leads take on real responsibilities — from running assemblies and representing the school, to organising charity events and mentoring younger students.

Talks & lecture series

Throughout the year, visiting speakers from universities, professions, and public life address the Sixth Form — from scientists and medics to lawyers, journalists, and business leaders. These talks broaden horizons and support students in making informed decisions about their futures.
